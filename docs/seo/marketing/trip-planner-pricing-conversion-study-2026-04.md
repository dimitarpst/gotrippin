# Trip planners — pricing, packaging & conversion paths (deep-dive log)

**Date:** 2026-04-04  
**Scope:** How **similar products charge**, what **free tiers optimize for**, and how they **drive sign-up** (web vs app). Complements [landing-hero-competitive-study-2026-04.md](./landing-hero-competitive-study-2026-04.md) (positioning + hero formats).

**Method:** Public marketing pages + help/pricing URLs; figures **change** — re-verify before any pricing decisions.

---

## 1. Price bands (consumer / prosumer)

| Product | Free tier gist | Paid anchor (public, rounded) | Paid “job” |
|---------|----------------|----------------------------------|------------|
| **TripIt** | Core itinerary from email | **~$49/yr** Pro ([tripit.com/web/pro](https://www.tripit.com/web/pro)) | Flight alerts, airport maps, seat/fare trackers — **ops for frequent flyers** |
| **Tripsy** | Broad free feature set (incl. guests, forward email) | **$59/yr**, **$299 lifetime** ([tripsy.app/pro](https://tripsy.app/pro)); support docs also cite monthly | **Apple-native** power: stats, docs, calendar, flight alerts |
| **Wanderlog** | “Free forever” planning + collab ([help center](https://help.wanderlog.com/hc/en-us/articles/13302997563547-Is-Wanderlog-free)) | Commonly cited **~$5/mo or ~$30/yr** Pro (verify on [wanderlog.com/pro](https://wanderlog.com/pro)) | Maps export, Gmail link, offline/power features |
| **Planors** | **3 trips**, **5 collaborators** | **$9/mo** Pro, **$29/mo** Business ([planors.com](https://www.planors.com/)) | Unlimited trips, more seats, budgeting/files |
| **SquadTrip** (group) | Free “Starter” | **~$29/mo** Launch tier cited in roundups — verify live | Group trip coordination / events skew |

**Pattern:**  
- **Itinerary incumbents** (TripIt, Tripsy): free is **generous on storage/sync**; paid is **alerts, exports, stats, family share**.  
- **Explicit group-SaaS** (Planors): free is **metered by trips + seats** — directly monetizes **collaboration scale**.

## 2. What the paid tier *means* strategically

| Archetype | Paid promise | gotrippin parallel (when monetizing) |
|-----------|--------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Operational insurance** | “Don’t miss gates / price drops / check-in” | Only if you lean into **travel-day** reliability (notifications, live status) |
| **Power user / ecosystem** | Calendar, docs, stats, lifetime on Apple | If you double down on **native apps** + habit |
| **Collaboration scale** | More collaborators, trips, voting | Natural if **crew** is the core wedge |
| **AI / automation** | Premium “smart” features | Aligns with **assistant**; avoid over-promising early |

TripIt Pro is **not** “pay to plan together” — it’s **pay to survive the trip**. Wanderlog Pro is **pay for convenience** on top of a strong free core. Planors is **pay for group size and seriousness**.

## 3. Conversion paths (funnel shape)

Observations from prior page reads + category norms:

| Pattern | Who | Implication |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| **Web: “Start planning” → in-browser trip** | Wanderlog, Triplanly, Planors | Low friction; **web is product**, mobile is companion |
| **Web: marketing → “Get the app” / store** | Tripsy, TripIt (strong) | **App is product**; web validates and converts to install |
| **Email capture / account first** | Mixed | Early gotrippin already web + auth — closer to **planner SaaS** than pure App Store shell |

**Takeaway:** gotrippin’s **web-first** stack matches **Wanderlog / collab tools** more than **Tripsy’s app-first** story. Pricing narrative later can emphasize **shared trips and route truth** (scale/team) rather than **airport alerts** unless you build that layer.

---

## 4. Free-tier design tensions (for product roadmap)

1. **Unlimited free + vague Pro** (Wanderlog-style): great growth; need **clear** premium value.  
2. **Metered collaborators** (Planors-style): easy to explain; risk feeling **cheap** if limits hit during real trips.  
3. **Lifetime purchase** (Tripsy): cash upfront; shifts support expectations — rare for young SaaS.

For an early **gotrippin**, a **generous free core** (1–N trips, small group) + **one obvious paid axis** (e.g. more collaborators, advanced AI budget, or export) keeps story simple. Avoid **12 dimensions** of limits.
