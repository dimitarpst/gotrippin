# Hero line research — “Plan trips together without the chaos”

**Brand lock (stakeholder, 2026-04-03):** Keep the **chaos** line as the signature; exact headline on `/home` is the two-line **Plan trips together** / **without the chaos.** (Low competition in web search for that phrase — good for recall.) Optional later: drop “together” if you want a shorter H1 — confirm before changing live copy.

**Question:** Is this line too generic? What are sensible alternatives?

## Verdict (short)

- **Partly category-generic, partly distinctive.** “Plan trips together” sits in the same neighborhood as competitors (“plan trips together, effortlessly”, “travel planning, together”). Many products sound similar at that level.
- **“Without the chaos” is the differentiator.** It names a *specific pain* (messy coordination, too many tools, group friction) instead of another abstract positive (“effortless”, “simple”, “smart”). That makes the line **more memorable and searchable as a phrase** than “smart trip planner” alone — even though it is not a product category name like “CRM.”
- **Tradeoff:** The line does **not** communicate map/route/AI. That is OK if the **next line or visual** carries product truth (your Bento block does part of that job).

## Competitor / adjacent phrasing (public sites)

| Product / site   | Hero-style line                          | Pattern                          |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Plan Harmony    | “The smartest way to plan your next trip” | Outcome + superlative            |
| Awaii           | “Travel planning, together. Made simple.” | Together + simplicity            |
| eHalo           | “Plan trips together, effortlessly”       | Together + low friction          |
| Plan Roam (App Store) | “Plan Smarter, Roam Together”       | Together + “smart”               |

**Takeaway:** “Together” is crowded. **Pairing it with a named enemy (“chaos”) or a concrete object (“one map”, “one itinerary”)** reduces “template SaaS” feel.

## Directions if you want alternatives (same spirit)

1. **Pain + together (keep chaos family)**  
   - “Plan together — without the group-chat mess.”  
   - “One shared trip. Less spreadsheet chaos.”

2. **Outcome + alignment (less emotional)**  
   - “Get everyone on the same itinerary.”  
   - “Build one trip everyone can see.”

3. **Honesty-first (if mockup/UI is simplified)**  
   - Lead with what is **actually** live: map + AI assistant + (future) sharing — short clause each.

4. **Avoid unless intentional**  
   - Internal jargon (“route-first”, “sequence”) — weak for search and memory.  
   - Pure superlative (“smartest”, “best”) — undifferentiated.

## SEO note

- Primary queries will still be things like **trip planner**, **group trip planner**, **shared itinerary**, **plan a trip with friends** — not your exact hero sentence.  
- The hero line’s job is **clarity + recall + emotional fit**; **title/H1/meta** should still include 1–2 plain keywords (see `docs/seo/seo.todo` Phase 3–4).
